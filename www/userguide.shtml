A User's Guide to Origins of Inbred Mice

Introduction

The purpose of this User's Guide is to assist you in using all of the features of the electronic version of Origins of Inbred Mice.

Navigation

There are several ways to navigate through this book:

  • The Table of Contents
  • The Subject Index
  • The abbreviated Table of Contents in the left column of most pages
  • The Previous and Next links at the top and bottom of most pages
  • Internal links within the body of the text

The Table of Contents lists Chapter headings. Click on any link in the Table of Contents to go to that section of the text.

The Subject Index list topics alphabetically. Click on any link to go to that Table, Figure, or section of the text.

See Chapter 1 for an example of the abbreviated Table of Contents and the Previous and Next links.

Special Features

References: Citations within the body of the text are linked to the appropriate reference in the References section. Many of the references are also in the body of literature curated by Mouse Genome Informatics. These will have a link to the citation in MGI below the citation in the References section. Clicking the link to MGI will take you to the references detail page at MGI. For example, Keeler (1931) is also curated at MGI. Clicking the link to MGI lets you search for Genes, Markers and Phenotypes, Marker Mapping Information, or Molecular Probes and Segments in MGI from this reference. This page also links to the citation in PubMed. Other references are not curated at MGI, but are curated in PubMed. For example, Potter and Lieberman (1967) is such a citation. Clicking the link to PubMed take you to the page containing the Abstract and several useful search features: click Related Articles to see references that may be more up to date, or Books to see chapters from reference works related to the subject of the reference.

Genes and Alleles: All mouse genes mentioned in the text are linked to MGI pages containing information on those genes, including current nomenclature. The link may be to a Gene Detail page, such as that for the gene referred to in the text as the albino (c) locus. The link may also be to an Allele Detail page, such as that for the c6H allele.

Tables: Clicking a reference to a Table within the text will cause that Table to appear in a new browser window (see, for example, Table 11-1). All Tables are listed on the Tables page.

Figures: Clicking a reference to a Figure within the text will cause that Figure to appear in a new browser window (see, for example, Figure 15-1). All Figures are listed on the Figures page.

Subject Index: The Subject Index from the book has links to the relevant section of the text, or to tables and figures, in place of the page numbers found in the book as it was originally published.

A Note on Printing

To print a section of the text of this book from your browser, you will have to navigate to that section using the Table of Contents. Use the abbreviated Table of Contents in the left column to go to the Table of Contents. From there, select the section that you would like to print. When that section appears in your browser window, click the frame containing that section to select it, then use your browser's Print Frame function. To print a Table or Figure, select the browser window in which it appears and use your browser's Print function.
